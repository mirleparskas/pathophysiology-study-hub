Official 12.3 learning outcomes covering menstrual, testicular, prostatic, gynecologic, sexually transmitted, and breast disorders.

Official 12.3 Learning Outcome Coverage

Build statusAll seven learning-outcome groups and assigned McCance readings are mapped. Detailed study notes, comparisons, and practice questions can be added as course materials arrive.

1. Disorders of Menstruation

LO
Explain the pathophysiology of common disorders of menstruation.
LO
Describe the clinical manifestations of menstrual disorders.
LO
Explain the relationship between endocrine abnormalities and the development of polyendocrine metabolic ovarian syndrome (formerly polycystic ovarian syndrome).
LO
Describe the clinical manifestations of polyendocrine metabolic ovarian syndrome.
Hormonal and Menstrual Alterations
McCance 8th ed.: pp. 759–766  |  McCance 9th ed.: pp. 770–776

2. Disorders of the Testis

LO
Compare and contrast the pathophysiological mechanisms and clinical manifestations of hydrocele, varicocele, spermatocele, testicular torsion, and cryptorchidism.
Disorders of the Scrotum, Testis, and Epididymis
McCance 8th ed.: pp. 841–844  |  McCance 9th ed.: pp. 834–839

3. Prostate Disorders

LO
Explain the pathophysiology of prostatitis, benign prostatic hyperplasia, and prostate cancer.
LO
Describe the clinical manifestations of prostatitis, benign prostatic hyperplasia, and prostate cancer.
Disorders of the Prostate Gland
McCance 8th ed.: pp. 846–859  |  McCance 9th ed.: pp. 843–853

4. Benign Growths of the Female Reproductive Organs

LO
Explain the pathophysiology of leiomyomas, endometriosis, benign ovarian cysts, and endometrial polyps.
LO
Describe the clinical manifestations of leiomyomas, endometriosis, benign ovarian cysts, and endometrial polyps.
Benign Growths and Proliferative Conditions
McCance 8th ed.: pp. 773–777  |  McCance 9th ed.: pp. 784–788

5. Cancers of the Female Reproductive System

LO
Identify risk factors for common gynecologic cancers, including cervical, endometrial, and ovarian cancer.
LO
Explain the pathophysiology of cervical, endometrial, and ovarian cancer.
LO
Discuss the relationship between human papillomavirus (HPV) infection and the development of cervical cancer.
LO
Describe the clinical manifestations of cervical, endometrial, and ovarian cancer.
Cancer and HPV
McCance 8th ed.: pp. 777–780, 782–786, 881  |  McCance 9th ed.: pp. 788–799, 881

6. Sexually Transmitted Infections

LO
Recognize causative microorganisms of HIV/AIDS, genital warts, syphilis, gonorrhea, and Chlamydia.
LO
Explain the pathophysiology of HIV/AIDS, herpes simplex virus infection, human papillomavirus infection, syphilis, gonorrhea, and Chlamydia.
LO
Describe the clinical manifestations of HIV/AIDS, herpes simplex virus infection, human papillomavirus infection, syphilis, gonorrhea, and Chlamydia.
LO
Discuss the relationship between sexually transmitted infections and pelvic inflammatory disease.
HIV/AIDS, Infection and Inflammation, and Sexually Transmitted Urogenital Infections
McCance 8th ed.: pp. 309–316, 767–771, 869–883  |  McCance 9th ed.: pp. 287–296, 777–780, 866–883

7. Disorders of the Breast

LO
Describe the characteristics of benign breast lesions, including non-proliferative lesions, proliferative lesions without atypia, and proliferative lesions with atypia.
LO
Explain the relationship between benign breast disease and breast cancer.
LO
Identify common risk factors for breast cancer.
LO
Explain the pathophysiology of breast cancer.
LO
Describe the clinical manifestations of breast cancer.
LO
Discuss the progression of breast cancer.
Breast Disorders and Breast Cancer
McCance 8th ed.: pp. 788–893  |  McCance 9th ed.: pp. 802–812
